How many people suffer from disabilities?

In order to determine whether or not a cause merits action, it must be proven that it is both relevant and widespread. According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, 61 million adults in the United States are living with a disability. That is over a quarter of the United States’ population, meaning about 1 in 4 people has a cognitive or physical disability. This shows that disabilities are very common in the United States and are thus a very relevant problem. With this data, we know that disabilities are widespread, but we also know that they cannot be prevented, so why then should we care about this cause and how could we help these people?

Accessibility

While disabilities are frequent in the United States, society is not designed or geared towards those who suffer from them. Technology, buildings, vehicles, parks, and many more things have been designed for decades without disabilities in mind, making them less accessible and causing those with disabilities to have even more struggles. In recent years, companies like Apple have been putting efforts toward making their products accessible which is an amazing leap forward in terms of accessibility in the United States. We can use videos such as “Sady” by Apple to motivate people to strive for accessibility for all people.

How Disabilities Affect Health

Click here to open this PDF in a new window


While accessibility is important, we also must consider the health of those who are disabled. As shown in the image, people with disabilities are more likely to have obesity, smoke, have heart disease, and diabetes than those who do not have disabilities. Risk for obesity, heart disease, and diabetes can be reduced with exercise and a great way to exercise is through sports. Accessibility in sports is not unheard of, but is not very common within communities, especially poorer ones. In order to make sports more available to those with disabilities, we can lobby and vote to allocate more resources towards making sports accessible as well as encouraging those who are disabled to participate in sports.

Helping disability people

As a special group in the society, the disabled live mainly in the community. This determines that the disabled have a closer relationship with the community, and even rely on the community. Disabled people cannot live without the conditions provided by the community for their daily necessities, such as food, clothing, housing and transportation, rehabilitation, education, employment and cultural life, participation in democratic life, exercise of democratic rights, display of talent and life values, and creation of social material and spiritual wealth.

Placement of disabled persons in Employment The fundamental solution to the problem of the disabled lies in employment, and the key to developing the cause of the disabled lies in employment. We should actively encourage and support individuals with disabilities to find employment and find jobs on their own. Actively contact with labor and employment department leaders and DPF to help them find jobs, so that they have a fixed income to improve their family life.

We will strengthen the organization building of disabled persons' federations and improve the network of organizations supporting the disabled.

Grassroots organizations such as volunteer liaison stations and service clubs for persons with disabilities have been set up to provide volunteers and funds to further improve the network of organizations for persons with disabilities so that they can enjoy services without leaving their communities

People with disabilities often encounter obstacles in their lives. Offer to help them solve them.

1. Persons with physical disabilities are restricted in their movements. Elevator buttons are too high to reach, making it impossible for people in wheelchairs and crutches to open doors by themselves, and stair steps are impassable.

2. An important reason why people with disabilities encounter barriers to mobility is that accessibility facilities are not fully functioning.

3. People with hearing and speech disorders cannot communicate with others normally. Because most people don't know sign language, there is an unbridgeable gap between the disabled and the normal people.

1. Improve barrier-free facilities in public leisure places, and make the elevator button lower; The steps are flatter; The blind path is wider.

2. People with hearing disabilities are eager to communicate with others. As long as you know some simple sign language, it is not difficult for you to communicate with deaf people. In particular, public service workers such as banks and post offices can learn sign language or open sign language Windows, which will greatly facilitate the disabled.

3. Most disabled people don't want to be pitied because it doesn't help. Therefore, we should pay special attention to their words and deeds, and try to treat them in the same way as healthy people.

International Disability Alliance


This is the logo for the International Disability Alliance which aims to protect the rights of people with disabilities and also support organizations of people with disabilities to hold their governments accountable and advocate for change locally, nationally and internationally. This organization really helps put into perspective how people with disabilities face hardships and are at a disadvantage. This shows we all need to pitch in and be aware of the difficulties and discrimination they face and we all can help little by little. Fueling change can be as simple as being mindful of the situations that could arise and being understanding of where people with disabilities come from and being an ally for them.
